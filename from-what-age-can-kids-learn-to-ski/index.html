From what age can kids learn to ski?

Tina Kinkead tips age learn 23 Jan 2017

From what age can kids learn to ski? Children can start on skis from about 2 years old, at this age it simply starts with learning to balance on absolutely flat snow on a pair of skis, at this age it is a very slow process, so be prepared with patience! The 2-3 year old child will get bored or exhausted with the activity quite quickly so it best to start out slow, if the child gets enough, it’s better to stop for the day or have a break as it’s meant to be fun not a chore. It’s never pleasant to see crying children not having fun, as it’s meant to be fun!

once the child is 4-5 they usually have motorskills that allow them to learn the basics, but it can still require more that a weeks practice to learn the snowplow, and it can easily take more than one week for those little legs to get strength enough to master the snowplow position. Also at this age half a ski day can be more that enough, it’s better to prioritise a short and fun experience than long days that end in exhaustion and tears.

from 6 and up children often learn the skiing quite quickly, they have the motorskills and leg strength to manage a half to a whole day of skiing, with breaks of course. They are skilled enough enough to understand the instructions and will often figure a lot out on their own initiative and curiosity.
